What is a force?

👊

Push force

When you apply a force in the direction away from your body, you are pushing. A push changes the position, speed, or direction of an object by acting outward from the source of the force.

Away from you
🤝

Pull force

When you apply a force in the direction toward your body, you are pulling. A pull draws an object closer to the source, changing its motion in the process.

Toward you
📐

Newton's First Law

An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion — unless a force acts on it. Both push and pull forces can break this state of rest or change the direction of motion.

Foundation law
⚖️

Newton's Second Law

The acceleration of an object depends on the mass of the object and the amount of force applied. Formulated globally as F = ma, meaning Acceleration = Net Force / Mass.

Unit: Newton (N)

Real-world examples

Push and pull forces are everywhere in daily life.

🚪

Opening a door

You push the door away from you to open it outward.

⚽

Kicking a ball

Your foot pushes the ball forward with force.

🛒

Pushing a cart

You push a shopping cart forward along the aisle.

🧲

Magnet & iron

A magnet pulls iron objects toward it from a distance.

🌍

Gravity

Earth pulls every object toward its center constantly.

🏹

Pulling a bow

You pull the bowstring back, storing energy to launch an arrow.

Key facts to remember

1

Force has direction

Every force acts in a specific direction. Pushing moves things away; pulling brings them closer. The direction of the force determines the direction of motion.

2

Force has magnitude

The strength (magnitude) of a force determines how much the object accelerates. A stronger force causes faster acceleration. Measured in Newtons (N).

3

Forces can balance

When two equal forces act in opposite directions, they cancel out and the object stays still. This is called balanced forces or equilibrium.

4

Friction is a force too

Friction is a push-like force that opposes motion. When you slide a book across a table, friction pushes back against the direction of movement, slowing it down.
